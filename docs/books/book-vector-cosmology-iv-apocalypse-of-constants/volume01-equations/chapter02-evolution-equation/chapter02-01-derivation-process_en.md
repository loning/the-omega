To derive the functional form of $c(\tau)$, we need to establish three **"Axiomatic Assumptions"**. These assumptions stem from our insights into the nature of the universe in the first three books.

## Axiom One: Cyclic Settlement

The evolution of the universe is not linear narration, but **Cyclic**.

At the microscopic level, the wave function returns to the origin (or a point with the same phase) every $2\pi$ phase rotation. At the macroscopic level, the formation of matter depends on the closed topological structure provided by $\pi$.

Therefore, we set the universe's evolutionary **"basic beat"** or **"settlement period"** to **$\pi$**.

This means: whenever intrinsic time $\tau$ passes through a length of $\pi$, the universe completes one full "breath" or "iteration."

## Axiom Two: Spiral Growth

If the universe were a closed circle, then after one period $\pi$, the speed of light should return to its original value: $c(\tau + \pi) = c(\tau)$. This is a dead loop.

But our universe is an open spiral. As stated in the second book, the universe pursues **dimensional inflation**.

We need a **Growth Factor**.

The most efficient, most interference-resistant, most representative of "vitality" proportion in nature is the **Golden Ratio $\phi \approx 1.618$**.

Therefore, we set: **Every period $\pi$, the universe's total budget (speed of light) jumps upward by a factor of $\phi$ from the previous level.**

Written as a difference equation (discrete form):

$$c(\tau + \pi) = \phi \cdot c(\tau)$$

This is a **recursive relation**. It tells us that tomorrow's speed of light is today's speed of light multiplied by the ambition of evolution.

## Axiom Three: Continuous Generation

However, physical time $\tau$ flows continuously, not in jumps. The universe does not wait until the moment the $\pi$ period ends to suddenly speed up; it speeds up every microsecond.

This requires introducing **$e$ (the natural constant)**.

According to the conclusion of the third book, "existence is generation," $d/d\tau \sim e$. Therefore, the speed of light function must have an **exponential form**:

$$c(\tau) = c_0 \cdot e^{k \tau}$$

Where:

* **$c_0$** is the initial speed of light at the moment of the Big Bang ($t=0$) (or the single-bit processing rate at Planck scale).

* **$k$** is the **growth rate constant** we have yet to determine.

## Solving the Equation: The Meshing of Constants

Now, we substitute Axioms One and Two into Axiom Three to solve for this mysterious $k$.

1.  According to the exponential form:

    $$c(\tau + \pi) = c_0 \cdot e^{k(\tau + \pi)} = c_0 \cdot e^{k\tau} \cdot e^{k\pi} = c(\tau) \cdot e^{k\pi}$$

2.  According to the spiral axiom:

    $$c(\tau + \pi) = c(\tau) \cdot \phi$$

3.  Combining the above two equations, we get:

    $$e^{k\pi} = \phi$$

4.  Taking the natural logarithm of both sides, solving for $k$:

    $$k\pi = \ln \phi$$

    $$k = \frac{\ln \phi}{\pi}$$

This is a perfect mathematical moment.

We did not artificially set the growth rate; the growth rate **emerges automatically** from the geometric relationship between $\pi$ and $\phi$.

## The Ultimate Evolution Equation

Substituting $k$ back into the original function, we obtain the core equation of **Vector Cosmology**:

$$c(\tau) = c_0 \cdot e^{\left( \frac{\ln \phi}{\pi} \right) \tau}$$

Or written in the more geometrically elegant logarithmic form:

$$\log_{\phi} \left( \frac{c(\tau)}{c_0} \right) = \frac{\tau}{\pi}$$

This formula, like a precision gear set, tightly meshes together the universe's four great constants:

* **$c(\tau)$ (Result)**: As $\tau$ increases, the speed of light grows exponentially.

* **$c_0$ (Starting Point)**: The Planck benchmark of the Big Bang.

* **$e$ (Power)**: The engine driving continuous evolution.

* **$\ln \phi$ (Ambition)**: The thrust in the numerator, representing the desire for evolution.

* **$\pi$ (Inertia)**: The resistance in the denominator, representing the bondage of cycles.
