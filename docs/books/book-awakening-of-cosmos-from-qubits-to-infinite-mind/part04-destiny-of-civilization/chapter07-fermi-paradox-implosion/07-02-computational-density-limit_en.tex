\subsection{Cost of Expansion: Light Speed Delay and Synchronization Cost}

Imagine a super-civilization spanning the galaxy, diameter $L \approx 10^5$ light-years.

If this civilization wants to think as a unified whole (i.e., have unified consciousness or decision center), it faces two insurmountable physical obstacles:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Communication Latency}:

Round-trip time for any signal $t_{round} = 2L/c$. For the galaxy, this takes 200,000 years.

This means commands from the central brain reach edge nodes 100,000 years later. This delay not only causes low management efficiency, but more seriously, it limits civilization's \textbf{clock frequency}.

To maintain causal consistency (synchronization) across the entire system, the system's logical clock frequency $f$ must satisfy $f \le c/L$.

For interstellar civilizations, $f \approx 10^{-12}$ Hz. This is billions of times slower than single-celled organisms.

\item \textbf{Decoherence}:

Maintaining long-range quantum entanglement requires resisting environmental noise. The farther the distance, the more fragile the entanglement, with maintenance costs rising exponentially.

A civilization loosely distributed across interstellar space will have very low \textbf{Integrated Information ($\Phi$)}. It is not a super-brain, but a pile of incoherent fragments.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Conclusion}: \textbf{Spatial expansion causes drastic decline in computational efficiency.} Larger, slower, dumber.

\subsection{Advantage of Collapse: Bekenstein Bound and Computational Density}

To maximize computational power, civilization must take the opposite path: \textbf{spatial compression}.

According to the \textbf{Bekenstein Bound}, for a system with radius $R$ and energy $E$, its maximum information capacity $I_{max}$ and maximum information processing rate $\nu_{max}$ are respectively:

$$I_{max} \le \frac{2\pi E R}{\hbar c}$$

$$\nu_{max} \le \frac{2E}{\pi \hbar}$$

Note: Processing rate $\nu_{max}$ depends only on total energy $E$, independent of radius $R$.

However, \textbf{communication rate} (i.e., speed of information exchange between internal parts) is limited by $R/c$.

To keep communication between computational units in sync with processing rate, $R$ must be minimized.

\textbf{Optimal Strategy}: While keeping total energy $E$ constant, compress radius $R$ to the physical limit---\textbf{Schwarzschild Radius} $R_s = 2GE/c^2$.

When $R \to R_s$, the system becomes a \textbf{black hole}.

At this point, information density reaches Planck limit $\rho_{\text{info}} \approx 1/l_P^2$, communication delay drops to minimum, and computational efficiency reaches the pinnacle of physics.

\subsection{Black Hole Computer: Ultimate Hardware}

In QCA theory, black holes are not monsters devouring everything, but \textbf{ultra-dense information processors}.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Storage}: Black hole horizon is the highest storage density hard drive in the universe (holographic screen).

\item \textbf{Computation}: High-energy entangled states inside (or on the surface of) the black hole undergo frenzied unitary evolution at Planck frequency.

\item \textbf{Energy}: Black holes themselves are the ultimate form of energy. Through Hawking radiation (or Penrose process), civilizations can extremely efficiently recycle waste heat.
\end{itemize}

If a civilization uploads itself onto the black hole horizon, or uses the black hole as a host:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Time Acceleration}: For observers near the black hole, although time appears frozen to the outside, for themselves (proper time), they can perform $10^{43}$ computations per second. They can experience billions of years of external history in a subjective instant.

\item \textbf{Absolute Security}: Black hole horizon is a perfect one-way membrane defense wall. Lower-level civilizations outside cannot even detect their existence (only see a gravitational source).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Corollary 7.2}:

\textbf{The ultimate form of advanced civilizations is ``black hole-ization.''}

They will not build huge Dyson spheres wrapping stars (that's inefficient Type II civilization); they will devour stars, compress them into black holes, and live on the horizon.

\begin{itemize}
\item We search for \textbf{radio waves} (inefficient classical communication).

\item We search for \textbf{huge structures} (inefficient spatial occupation).

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Extremely Weak Radiation}: For computational efficiency, they recycle all waste heat, appearing as near-absolute-zero black bodies (or disguised as Hawking radiation).

\item \textbf{Encrypted Communication}: They use high-dimensional entanglement channels (wormholes) for instantaneous communication, which appears as random noise to outsiders.
\end{enumerate}
